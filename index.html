01 — Introduction

What is Intellectual Property?

Intellectual Property (IP) refers to legal rights granted to individuals or organizations over creations of the mind. These creations may include inventions, literary and artistic works, designs, symbols, names, images, and software.
IP protection ensures that creators can control and benefit from their innovations while promoting economic growth, technological advancement, and creative industries.
In the digital era, especially in IT, understanding IP is essential. Developers, designers, entrepreneurs, and students constantly create and use digital content, making awareness of IP laws critical.
Core Categories
  • Copyright
  • Patent
  • Trademark

03 — Types of Intellectual Property (C, P, T.)

© Copyright

Automatically protects original works of authorship fixed in tangible form — from the moment of creation. No registration required.
Software Code Graphic Design Music & Film Websites Books & Research
Lasts life of the author + ~70 years. Covers expression of ideas, not the underlying idea itself.
Key Characteristics
  • Protection is automatic upon creation
  • Covers expression of ideas, not the ideas themselves
  • Copying code without permission = infringement
  • Redistributing proprietary software = infringement
  • Fair use doctrine allows limited educational use

04 — Types of Intellectual Property (C, P, T.)

⚙ Patent

Protects inventions and technical solutions to problems. Grants the inventor exclusive rights to make, use, or sell the invention for a limited period (~20 years).
Utility Patent
New processes, machines, systems — the most common type.
Design Patent
Ornamental design or unique appearance of a product.
Requirements
  • Novelty — must be new and original
  • Non-obviousness — not trivial to experts
  • Industrial applicability — practical use
  • Must apply — not automatic like copyright
  • In IT: covers hardware, algorithms & architectures

05 — Types of Intellectual Property (C, P, T.)

™ Trademark

Protects brand identifiers — names, logos, slogans, product names, and symbols. Purpose: prevent consumer confusion and protect brand identity.
Business Names Logos Slogans Product Names Symbols
Unlike patents, trademarks can last indefinitely as long as they remain in active use and registration is maintained.
Key Facts
  • Prevents consumer confusion in the market
  • Protects brand identity and market reputation
  • Can exist as common law (unregistered) or registered
  • Registration is recommended for full protection
  • Must conduct clearance search before launch

06 — Ethics

Plagiarism

Presenting another person's work, ideas, or expressions as your own without proper acknowledgment.
Forms of Plagiarism
  • Copy-pasting text without citation
  • Submitting downloaded assignments
  • Reusing code without attribution
  • Paraphrasing without credit
Consequences
Academic Institutions
May result in failing grades, Suspension, or Expulsion.
Professional Environments
Loss of employment, Legal liability, Professional blacklisting.
In software development, copying proprietary code or failing to acknowledge open-source contributors can lead to legal disputes.

07 — Licensing

Open Source Code

// Scenario: Your company builds closed-source (Proprietary) software.
// You want to use a free open-source library. Can you?

function canWeUseThisLibrary(ourSoftware, libraryLicense) {
    
    // GPL is a "Copyleft" license. It requires your software to also be open-source.
    if (ourSoftware === 'Proprietary' && libraryLicense === 'GPL') {
        // ❌ DANGER: You cannot use GPL code in closed-source software!
        return 'LAWSUIT RISK! Do not use.';
    }
    
    // MIT is "Permissive". It lets you do almost anything.
    if (libraryLicense === 'MIT') {
        // ✅ SAFE: Just keep the original copyright notice.
        return 'Safe to use commercially!';
    }
}
Misunderstanding open-source licensing is a major legal risk for startups. Compliance is mandatory.
MIT License
Most permissive. Use, modify, redistribute freely. Only keep copyright notice. Commercial use OK.
GNU GPL
Copyleft principle. Derivative works must also be GPL. Source stays open. Used by Linux kernel.
Apache 2.0
Business-friendly. Requires attribution. Grants patent protection. Used by Android, Kubernetes.

08 — Legal

Trademark Infringement

Using a mark identical or confusingly similar to a registered trademark without authorization.
Legal standard: "Likelihood of consumer confusion"
Common Examples
  • Logo confusingly similar to known brand
  • Brand name used in misleading context
  • Selling counterfeit goods
  • Mimicking product packaging (trade dress)
Penalties
Injunction
Immediate court order to halt infringing activity.
Damages
Financial compensation + destruction of goods.
Forced Rebrand
Startups must check trademark conflicts before launch — rebranding is extremely costly.

09 — Domain

Cybersquatting

Registering, trafficking, or using domain names identical or confusingly similar to established trademarks — typically for profit.
Common Motives
  • Reselling domain to trademark owner at inflated price
  • Redirecting legitimate web traffic illegally
  • Damaging brand reputation
  • Misleading consumers via lookalike domains
Consequences
  • Forced domain transfer
  • Financial penalties
  • Litigation costs
Legal Framework
ICANN UDRP
International dispute resolution. Rightful owners reclaim domains without lengthy litigation.
Anti-Cybersquatting Laws
Forced domain transfer · Financial penalties · Litigation costs.

Summary

Key Takeaways

Know Your Rights
Copyright protects your creative work automatically. Patent and trademark require deliberate registration and strategy.
Respect Others' IP
Plagiarism, license violations, and brand imitation have serious academic, professional, and legal consequences.
Act Proactively
Conduct IP audits, register trademarks before launch, review all open-source licenses, and consult legal counsel.
IP is a foundational element of modern digital and business ecosystems. Proper management safeguards innovation, ensures fair competition, and sustains long-term growth.
